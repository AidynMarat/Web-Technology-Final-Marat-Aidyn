Desserts Dishes Drinks
  • Yokan - Japanese sweetness that resembles a cross between a pastille and marmalade
  • Zenzai - traditional red bean soup adzuki with rice cakes
  • Sakuramochi - a rice cake of a delicate pink shade, just like sakura during the flowering period
  • Manju - boiled pies with sweet filling of sweet anko beans
  • Nagamashi - Rice sweets stuffed with sweet beans or fruit jelly, which are depicted as animals or plants
  • Unagi - is a river eel, usually grilled on coals and covered with a sweet barbecue or fish sauce
  • Kushiaki - different dishes cooked on a grill and on a spit, usually mushrooms. There is also a Yakitori dish - pieces of chicken of a certain type, also cooked on sticks on the grill
  • Gyoza - Japanese dumplings served separately or with a plate of delicious broth
  • Shabu-shabu - thin slices of marbled pork or beef served raw together with a pot of boiling broth right on the table
  • Sashimi - beautifully sliced and beautifully served pieces of usually raw seafood, such as fish fillets, shellfish, cephalopods, etc.
  • Sake - is the pride of our restaurant and the whole of Japan. Rice alcoholic drink. Yamadanishiki is the "king of rice", considered the best of all types of sake
  • Shochu - is an alcoholic drink made from rice, sweet potatoes, barley, buckwheat or sugar cane, affected by noble mold
  • Awamori - is a variety of shochu, which is produced on the island of Okinawa.
